Bioremediation Methods

  • Microbial Degradation: Uses bacteria and fungi to break down pollutants.
  • Phytoremediation: Uses plants to absorb or contain contaminants.
  • Mycoremediation: Uses fungi to degrade toxic substances.

FAQs

What is bioremediation?

It's the use of microorganisms to break down environmental pollutants into less harmful forms.

Is bioremediation safe?

Yes, it's a natural process and generally safe when managed correctly.

Where is bioremediation used?

It's used in cleaning up oil spills, industrial sites, and areas contaminated with heavy metals or pesticides.
